TIL: Why Having a Blog Matters More Than I Thought

What I was doing

Setting up this blog, wiring up categories, posts, metadata validation, slug routing, and at some point I stopped and asked myself: why am I building this at all?

What I discovered

A blog is a long-running public record of how you think. Not just what you know, but how you reason, what you find interesting, and how that changes over time. That is different from a portfolio, a resume, or a GitHub profile.

Writing forces clarity in a way that thinking alone does not. You can hold a fuzzy understanding of something in your head indefinitely. The moment you try to write it down, the gaps show up immediately. The act of publishing raises the stakes just enough to make you fill those gaps.

There is also a compounding effect. A single post is nearly worthless. Fifty posts written consistently over a year become a searchable, linkable record of your growth that works for you while you are not. People find it, reference it, and form an impression of you before they have ever spoken to you.

Why it matters

Most knowledge work is invisible. Code gets merged and forgotten, decisions get made in calls that leave no trace. A blog makes your thinking visible and permanent, which is useful both for others and for your future self.

It also lowers the barrier to starting conversations. A post you wrote six months ago can be the reason someone reaches out today.

Example

This blog itself. The investment in building the routing, the validation, the slug logic, none of that matters if nothing gets written. The infrastructure is only as valuable as what it publishes.
